First space lettuce grown on the ISS

August 10th

Veg-01


Credit: NASA

  • Nickname

    VEGGIE

  • Type

    Deployable plant growth unit

  • Goal

    "To provide the crew with a palatable, nutritious, and safe source of fresh food and a tool to support relaxation and recreation" [1]

On August 10th, 2015, NASA astronauts ate some of the first ever space-grown lettuce.

The red romaine lettuce took 33 days to grow in what in NASA's Veggie plant growth facility. This accomplishment is a huge step in exploring the future of space missions on other planets, since the ability to grow food outside of Earth would help extend the life of space missions in general.

While this doesn't mean we can head out to Mars now, it certainly bodes well for future missions to come.
