Philae wakes up briefly

June 13th

Philae


Credit: ESA

  • Launch Date

    March 2nd, 2004

  • Mass

    100 kg

  • Landing

    67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko (2014)

After landing on the comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko nearly a year prior and going into a deep sleep, the European spacecraft Philae woke up for the first time on June 13th, 2015. The spacecraft had sent roughly 60 hours of data on comet sniffing and prodding before falling asleep.

Part of Philae's mission was to learn about possible origins for planet Earth, such as the theory that comets had smashed into our planet and provided it with water and other chemicals. Philae had found several organic molecules on the comet, as well as four new molecules never found on a comet.
