On December 16th, 2015, Congress released a budget proposal for 2016 which included a budget of $19.3B for NASA, a $1.27B increase from the previous year.

The increase in spending comes long after criticism that the space agency was very underfunded, leading to the formation privately funded space programs such as SpaceX. In fact, the goal of the increased budget seems to be to end NASA's current dependence on Russia for rockets and other equipment. With the new money, NASA is focused on the Space Launch System (SLS), which will help send astronauts to deep space destinations easier. The funding also reaches over 10,000 US scientists through research awards.
