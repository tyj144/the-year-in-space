New Horizons flies by Pluto

July 14th

Credit: NASA

New Horizons


Credit: Kevin Gill

  • Launch Date

    January 19, 2006

  • Mass

    478 kg

  • Major Flybys

    Moon (2006), Jupiter (2007), Pluto (2015)

On July 14th, 2015, New Horizons became the first ever spacecraft to reach the dwarf planet Pluto.

Launched on January 19th, 2006, the spacecraft took more than six years to reach the icy dwarf, a journey which involved swinging past Jupiter for a gravity boost and studying Pluto's nearby moons. As the first spacecraft to travel to the edge of our solar system, New Horizons has found information never before known, such as the discovery of four new moons orbiting Pluto. The spacecraft has also taken some of the best pictures we have of these far away planets, showing a stunning and detailed icy landscape.


An image of Pluto's surface taken by New Horizons.
Credit: NASA/Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory

NASA plans on having New Horizons continue exploring further and into the Kuiper belt, where it will hopefully answer questions about the origin of our solar system.
