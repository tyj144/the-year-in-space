Scott Kelly and Mikhail Kornienko begin one-year space mission

March 27th

Scott Kelly

NASA Astronaut

Mikhail Kornienko

Russian Cosmonaut

On March 27th 2015, two astronauts, NASA astronaut Scott Kelly and Russian cosmonaut Mikhail Kornienko, were launched to the ISS (International Space Station) for a one year mission to better understand the effects of space on the human body. This research has profound implications, because it will provide more more information on how NASA will explore future manned space missions.

While the two are in space, NASA is conducting studies on the ground with retired astronaut Mark Kelly, Scott Kelly's twin. These studies will involve many psychological and physical tests and will serve as a comparison to the effects that his twin experiences in space.

A year in space can have incredible effects on people. When speaking to TIME magazine, Kelly said he almost forgot what life was like on Earth. “You definitely feel like you’re here and everyone else that’s important to you and everything that you care about and love is down there.”
