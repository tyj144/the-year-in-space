Dawn spacecraft arrives at Ceres, becomes first to orbit a dwarf planet

March 6th

The Dawn spacecraft


Credit: NASA/JPL
  • Launched
    September 27th, 2007
  • Mission duration
    ~9 years
  • Launch site
    Cape Canaveral Air Force Station
    Florida, USA

On March 6th, 2015, NASA's Dawn spacecraft arrived at Ceres, the dwarf planet which lies between Mars and Jupiter. Once the spacecraft was around 38,000 miles away from the planet, it was pulled in by the planet's gravity and became the first spacecraft to ever orbit a dwarf planet.

Dawn's orbit of Ceres comes shortly after exploring a giant asteroid Vesta, both of which are the largest bodies between Mars and Jupiter. The spacecraft has taken more than 16,000 pictures which have let us learn more about the worlds that have been shrouded in mystery.
