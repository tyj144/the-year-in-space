On September 28th, 2015, people across various parts of the world went outside to witness a supermoon lunar eclipse, which many know as the "Blood Moon."

A supermoon lunar eclipse occurs very rarely, happening around ever 20 or so years. The name supermoon is used because the moon is at its closest point to the Earth, making it appear 14% larger and 33% brighter than normal. In general, this phenomena is caused by how lunar eclipses occur. When a lunar eclipse happens, the Earth blocks the light of the Sun from hitting the Moon and casts a dark shadow over it. During this eclipse, however, some sunlight can scatter off the Earth's atmosphere, giving the mood a shade of red.
